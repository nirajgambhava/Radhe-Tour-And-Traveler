Jaggnath

The world-famous Lord Jagannath Puri Rath Yatra is an important annual festival held in India. As per the traditional calendar, the Puri Rath Yatra begins on the Dwitiya Tithi in Ashadha, Shukla Paksha. Currently, it falls in the month of June or July in the Gregorian calendar.

    The Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, is one of the most famous temples dedicated to Lord Jagannath. It is an important pilgrimage destination and attracts millions of devotees every year, especially during the Rath Yatra festival.

    Delhi has a well-connected transportation system, including the Delhi Metro, buses, auto-rickshaws, taxis, and cycle rickshaws. The Delhi Metro is one of the most convenient and efficient ways to travel around the city, covering various tourist spots and neighborhoods.

Goa

Goa: Where the sun, sea, and serenity come together.

    Goa has a rich cultural heritage influenced by its history of Portuguese colonization. Visitors can explore colonial-era churches, cathedrals, and forts, such as Basilica of Bom Jesus, Se Cathedral, and Fort Aguada. The state also celebrates numerous festivals, including the Feast of St. Francis Xavier, Carnival, and Shigmo, which showcase its diverse cultural traditions.

Dwarka

May your temple moments be etched in the sands of time.

    The Dwarkadhish Temple, also known as the Jagat Mandir, is the main attraction in Dwarka. It is dedicated to Lord Krishna and is believed to have been built over 2500 years ago by Krishna's grandson, Vajranabha. The temple is a major pilgrimage destination and attracts devotees from all over India and abroad.

Kachh

“Kutch Nahi Dekha to Kutch Nahi Dekha” The slogan rings in my head as I imagine Mr. Amitabh bachchan addressing with the backdrop of the famous white desert of Kutch.

    Kutch is known for its unique geographical features, including the Great Rann of Kutch and the Little Rann of Kutch. The Rann of Kutch is a vast salt marsh that becomes submerged in water during the monsoon season and transforms into a white desert during the dry season. The region is also characterized by its semi-arid climate and barren landscapes.

Somnath

In the heart of devotion, lies the beauty of Somnath Temple. .

    The Somnath Temple is the main attraction in Somnath and is considered one of the holiest shrines in Hinduism. It is believed to have been originally built by the moon god Soma (hence the name "Somnath") and later reconstructed by various rulers, including the Chalukyas, the Mughals, and the Marathas. The current temple structure, built in the traditional Chalukyan style of architecture, was completed in 1951 under the patronage of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel.
